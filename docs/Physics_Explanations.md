# Physics Explanations and Artifact Risks

This file provides conventional interpretation lanes for reported SEG bench effects. It does not prove or disprove the full SEG claim stack.

## Contactless Motion Near Spinning Copper

A magnet near a moving conductor may experience forces or torques through ordinary electromagnetic coupling. Eddy currents, Lenz-law behavior, field geometry, drag, vibration, and gap changes may all create visible motion.

The MVP does not ask whether motion can happen. It asks whether a conditioned magnet behaves measurably differently from unconditioned and sham-conditioned controls under identical conditions.

## Magnetic Field Patterning

Field maps, Hall sensor scans, gauss meter readings, and repeatable geometry are required before any patterning claim is treated as more than visual or subjective observation.

Magnetic viewing film may be useful as a visual aid, but it is not enough by itself.

## Mechanical Resonance

Small magnets and fixtures may have mechanical resonance modes. A resonance observation does not prove energy gain or anomalous physics. It must be measured with controlled sweeps and sensor data.

## Dielectric / Electret Concepts

Charged dielectric materials may retain charge under some conditions. Charge retention must be measured over time with appropriate instrumentation.

## Design Heuristics

Any numerical or geometric design rule in this project is treated as a design heuristic until outcomes are measured. The protocol validates observed behavior, not the heuristic itself.

## Artifact Risks

Formal tests must guard against:

- airflow,
- vibration,
- static charge,
- motor harmonics,
- fixture friction,
- camera frame-rate aliasing,
- hidden ferromagnetic parts,
- thermal drift,
- human hand proximity,
- rotor wobble or runout,
- sensor saturation,
- instrument drift,
- changing gap under load,
- expectation bias,
- selective trial exclusion.

## Extraordinary Claims

Energy generation, cooling, weight change, propulsion, flight, and over-unity operation require calibrated instruments, controls, uncertainty analysis, and independent replication. They are not validated by contactless motion alone.
